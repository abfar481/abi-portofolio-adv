Sukarno (Koesno Sosrodihardjo)

Indonesian statesman, orator, revolutionary, and nationalist who was the first president of Indonesia

Here's a time line of Sukarno life:

  • 1901 - Sukarno was born in Surabaya in the Dutch East Indies (now Indonesia), He was originally named Kusno Sosrodihardjo.
  • 1912 - Graduating from a native primary school
  • 1916 - Sukarno went to a Hogere Burgerschool (a Dutch type higher level secondary school) in Surabaya, where he met Tjokroaminoto, a nationalist and founder of Sarekat Islam.
  • 1920 - Sukarno married Tjokroaminoto's daughter Siti Oetari.
  • 1921 - He began to study civil engineering (with a focus on architecture) at the Technische Hoogeschool te Bandoeng (Bandoeng Institute of Technology)
  • 1923 - Sukarno divorced Siti Oetari to marry Inggit, Sukarno later divorced Inggit and married Fatmawati
  • 1926 - He obtained an Ingenieur degree (abbreviated as "ir.", a Dutch type engineer's degree)
  • 1927 - Sukarno with his friends from the Algemeene Studieclub established a pro-independence party
  • 1929 - Sukarno and other key PNI leaders were arrested by Dutch colonial authorities in a series of raids throughout Java
  • 1930 - Sukarno made a series of long political speeches attacking colonialism and imperialism, titled Indonesia Menggoegat. Sukarno was sentenced to four years in prison, which were served in Sukamiskin prison in Bandung
  • 1931 - Sukarno was released early on 31 December. By this time, he had become a popular hero widely known throughout Indonesia.
  • 1933 - Sukarno published a series of writings titled Mentjapai Indonesia Merdeka ("To Attain Independent Indonesia"). For this writing, he was arrested by Dutch police while visiting fellow nationalist Mohammad Hoesni Thamrin in Jakarta on 1 August
  • 1934 - Sukarno was shipped, along with his family (including Inggit Garnasih), to the remote town of Ende, on the island of Flores.
  • 1938 - Due to an outbreak of malaria in Flores, the Dutch authorities decided to move Sukarno and his family to Bencoolen (now Bengkulu) on the western coast of Sumatra, in February
  • 1942 - In February, Imperial Japan invaded the Dutch East Indies quickly defeating Dutch forces who marched, bussed and trucked Sukarno and his entourage three hundred kilometres from Bengkulu to Padang, Sumatra. In July, Sukarno was sent back to Jakarta, where he re-united with other nationalist leaders recently released by the Japanese, including Mohammad Hatta.
  • 1944 - with the war going badly for the Japanese, Prime Minister Kuniaki Koiso promised independence for Indonesia, although no date was set.
  • 1945 - with the fall of Philippines to American hands, the Japanese allowed for the establishment of the Investigating Committee for Preparatory Work for Independence (BPUPK), a quasi-legislature consisting of 67 representatives from most ethnic groups in Indonesia. Sukarno was appointed as head of the BPUPK and was tasked to lead discussions to prepare the basis of a future Indonesian state.
  • 1945 - In June, Sukarno introduced these five principles, known as pancasila, during the joint session of the BPUPK held in the former Volksraad Building (now called Gedung Pancasila).
  • 17 August 1945 - ukarno returned to his house at Jalan Pegangsaan Timur No. 56, where Mohammad Hatta joined him. Finally, at 10 am, Sukarno and Hatta stepped to the front porch, where Sukarno declared the independence of the Republic of Indonesia in front of a crowd of 500 people.

"Aku ini bukan apa-apa kalau tanpa rakyat. Aku besar karena rakyat, berjuang karena rakyat, dan aku penyambung lidah rakyat."

-- Sukarno
